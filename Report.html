Task 2 - AES

  1. Identify the Shannon diffusion element(s).
  2. Also identify the Shannon confusion element(s).
  1. Confusion: An encryption operation where the relationship between key and ciphertext is obscured.
    Today, a common element for achieving confusion is substitution.
  2. Diffusion: An encryption operation where the influence of one plaintext symbol is spread over many ciphertext symbols with the goal of hiding statistical properties of the plaintext.
    A simple diffusion element is the bit permutation.

Each round of AES encryption consists of four parts.
First, we perform byte substitution. This provides the element of confusion needed to hide the connection between plaintext and ciphertext.
Then we perform two diffusion functions: ShiftRows and MixColumns. Finally, we add the round’s subkey to the output.
The result is then transmitted to the next round of encryption. The final round of encryption performs the same operations but skips the MixColumns function. The final product is a 128-bit block of encrypted text.
